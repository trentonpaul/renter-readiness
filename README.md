# Renter Readiness Calculator

A single-page web tool that helps prospective renters assess their financial readiness before signing a lease. Users enter a handful of numbers and receive a weighted score across five key factors, along with a breakdown of where they stand on each one.

## What It Does

The calculator takes inputs across three areas — income & rent, personal situation, and savings — and produces a score from 0 to 100. The score falls into one of four tiers: **Excellent**, **Good**, **Getting There**, or **Needs Work**. Each factor includes a short explanation of the user's result and what the ideal range looks like.

Credit history is not scored but is flagged as an important variable that landlords will check independently, with links to check for free.

## Scoring Factors

The overall score is a weighted sum of five factors:

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---|
| Rent-to-Income ratio | 35% |
| Emergency Fund | 20% |
| Move Savings | 20% |
| Debt-to-Income ratio | 15% |
| Income Predictability | 10% |

Each factor is scored on a 0–100 scale based on standard financial health benchmarks. Rent-to-income carries the most weight as it is the primary signal landlords use during the approval process.

## Score Tiers

| Score | Tier |
|---|---|
| 90–100 | Excellent |
| 75–89 | Good |
| 50–74 | Getting There |
| 0–49 | Needs Work |

## Notes

- All monetary inputs are rounded to the nearest whole dollar
- Move-in cost targets are estimated automatically based on distance and whether the user plans to use professional movers, but can be overridden with a known figure
- Income predictability is framed around how reliably the user can forecast their income, not how they earn it
